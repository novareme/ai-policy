AI Communication Policy

Prime Brokerage & Swaps Infrastructure Division
Internal Use Only
v1.0 | Dec 2025

"How can leadership use generative AI responsibly and authentically in internal communication without eroding trust or accountability?"

Guiding Principles
T

Transparency

Substantive AI-assisted content must carry appropriate attribution when it materially influences decisions.

A

Accountability

Every communication sent under your name represents your professional endorsement—regardless of AI involvement.

D

Data Protection

Never enter client data, PII, or confidential information into non-approved external AI systems.

H

Human Voice

Sensitive matters, feedback, and significant decisions require genuine human voice that AI cannot replicate.

When Can I Use AI?

✓ Permitted (No Disclosure Required)

  • Grammar, spelling & style refinement
  • Code documentation for your own code
  • Meeting summary drafts (verified by you)
  • Templates for routine communications

⚠ Restricted (Disclosure Required)

  • Substantive analysis or recommendations
  • Research summaries from multiple sources
  • Risk assessments or compliance content
  • Client-facing materials

✕ Prohibited

  • Confidential data in external AI tools
  • Performance reviews & HR matters
  • Regulatory submissions (without Compliance)
  • Conflict resolution & grievances
5 Questions Before You Send
1

Does this involve confidential or client data?

2

Would the recipient expect my authentic voice?

3

Could AI errors cause harm or reputational damage?

4

Does this communication influence a key decision?

5

Am I comfortable being asked "Did you use AI?"

Before Sending
Read entire communication—does it represent your intent?
Verify all factual claims against authoritative sources
Check tone matches the relationship and context
Confirm technical accuracy of code/configurations
Remove AI artefacts (generic phrases, inconsistent style)
